Movie Review Analyzer: Build a simple sentiment analyzer and test on 5 reviews.

DATASET:

reviews = [
    "The movie was amazing and full of suspense",
    "I hated the film, it was boring and too long",
    "Fantastic performance by the lead actor",
    "The plot was dull and predictable",
    "An excellent movie with great visuals"
]

Program:

from textblob import TextBlob

# Reviews
reviews = [
    "The movie was amazing and full of suspense",
    "I hated the film, it was boring and too long",
    "Fantastic performance by the lead actor",
    "The plot was dull and predictable",
    "An excellent movie with great visuals"
]

# Analyze sentiment
for review in reviews:
    analysis = TextBlob(review)
    polarity = analysis.sentiment.polarity
    
    if polarity > 0:
        sentiment = "Positive"
    elif polarity < 0:
        sentiment = "Negative"
    else:
        sentiment = "Neutral"
    
    print(f"Review: {review}")
    print(f"Sentiment: {sentiment} (Score: {round(polarity,2)})\n")



Output:
Review: The movie was amazing and full of suspense
Sentiment: Positive (Score: 0.6)

Review: I hated the film, it was boring and too long
Sentiment: Negative (Score: -0.8)

Review: Fantastic performance by the lead actor
Sentiment: Positive (Score: 0.5)

Review: The plot was dull and predictable
Sentiment: Negative (Score: -0.6)

Review: An excellent movie with great visuals
Sentiment: Positive (Score: 0.7)

Explanation:

1.Words like “amazing”, “fantastic”, “excellent” → Positive sentiment
2.Words like “hated”, “boring”, “dull” → Negative sentiment
3.The model calculates a polarity score (-1 to +1):
       +1 → Very Positive
        0 → Neutral
       -1 → Very Negative
