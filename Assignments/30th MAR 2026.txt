Prompt Engineer: Write prompts for resume, business idea, study plan and compare weak vs strong prompts.



Prompt Engineering is the skill of writing clear, specific, and structured instructions to get the best response from an AI model.

1. Resume Prompt
Weak Prompt:
“Make my resume”

Strong Prompt:
“Create a professional resume for a final-year Electronics and Communication Engineering student with skills in Python, Java, and basic Full Stack development. Include sections: education, skills, projects, strengths, and career objective.”

Comparison:
Weak → Too vague
Strong → Clear role, skills, and structure


2. Business Idea Prompt
Weak Prompt:
“Give me a business idea”

Strong Prompt:
“Suggest 3 low-investment business ideas suitable for a college student in India, preferably in the water bottle or eco-friendly product niche. Include cost, target customers, and profit potential.”

Comparison:
Weak → No direction
Strong → Specific context and requirements

3. Study Plan Prompt
Weak Prompt:
“Make a study plan”

Strong Prompt:
“Create a 4-week study plan for learning Python basics for a beginner. Include daily topics, practice tasks, and revision time, assuming 2 hours per day.”

Comparison:
Weak → No timeline or goal
Strong → Structured and realistic

4. Project Prompt
Weak Prompt:
“Give project ideas”

Strong Prompt:
“Suggest 5 mini-project ideas for an Electronics and Communication Engineering student interested in AI and FPGA, with simple explanations and required tools.”

Comparison:
Weak → Too general
Strong → Tailored to domain and interest
