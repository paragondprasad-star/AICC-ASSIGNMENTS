Storytelling with Graphs: Create bar chart, pie chart, histogram and write a short data story explaining trends.

Program:

import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Data
categories = ['1-2 hrs', '2-3 hrs', '3-4 hrs', '4-5 hrs', '5+ hrs']
students = [8, 12, 15, 10, 5]

# 1. Bar Chart
plt.bar(categories, students)
plt.title("Students Study Hours (Bar Chart)")
plt.xlabel("Study Hours")
plt.ylabel("Number of Students")
plt.show()

# 2. Pie Chart
plt.pie(students, labels=categories, autopct='%1.1f%%')
plt.title("Study Hours Distribution (Pie Chart)")
plt.show()

# 3. Histogram
data = [1,2,2,3,3,3,4,4,4,5,2,3,4,1,2,3,4,5]
plt.hist(data, bins=5)
plt.title("Study Hours Histogram")
plt.xlabel("Study Hours")
plt.ylabel("Frequency")
plt.show()


The output graph show how many hours students study daily.
1.The bar chart shows that most students study 3–4 hours per day, while fewer students study more than 5 hours.
2.The pie chart shows that the largest portion of students fall in the 3–4 hour category, indicating moderate study habits.
3.The histogram shows the distribution of study hours, where most values cluster around 3–4 hours.

Overall, the data suggests that the majority of students maintain a balanced study routine, with relatively fewer students studying either very little or very long hours.
